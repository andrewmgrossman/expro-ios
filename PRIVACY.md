# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date: March 10, 2026**

This Privacy Policy describes the data practices of ExPro ("the App"), developed by Andrew M. Grossman ("Developer," "we," "us," or "our"). By downloading, installing, or using the App, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

## 1. Information We Collect

We do not collect, store, transmit, or process any personal data or personally identifiable information. The App does not require user accounts, registration, or login credentials of any kind.

## 2. Local Network Communication

The App communicates exclusively with Devialet hardware devices over your local network. All commands and data exchanged between the App and your Devialet device remain on your local network and are not transmitted to the Developer or any third party. The App requires local network access permissions solely for the purpose of discovering and controlling compatible devices on your network.

## 3. Analytics and Tracking

The App does not incorporate any analytics frameworks, advertising identifiers, tracking pixels, or similar technologies. We do not track your usage of the App in any manner.

## 4. Third-Party Services

The App does not integrate with any third-party services, APIs, or SDKs that collect user data. No data is shared with or sold to any third party.

## 5. Data Storage

Any preferences or configuration data generated by the App are stored locally on your device and are not accessible to the Developer or any third party. Such data is removed when the App is uninstalled.

## 6. Children's Privacy

The App does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under the age of 13. Because the App collects no data whatsoever, it is compliant with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA) and similar statutes.

## 7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We reserve the right to modify this Privacy Policy at any time. Any changes will be reflected by an updated effective date on this page. Your continued use of the App following the posting of changes constitutes acceptance of those changes.
